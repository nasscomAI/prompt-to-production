HR LEAVE POLICY SUMMARY (STRICT BINDING)
==================================================
Clause 1.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] This policy governs all leave entitlements for permanent and contractual employees of the City Municipal Corporation (CMC).
Clause 1.2: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] This policy does not apply to daily wage workers or consultants. Those categories are governed by their respective contracts.
Clause 2.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Each permanent employee is entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
Clause 2.2: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month from the date of joining.
Clause 2.3: 14-day advance notice MUST be provided for leave applications.
Clause 2.4: Written approval from direct manager MUST be received before leave commences. Verbal approval is invalid.
Clause 2.5: Unapproved absence WILL be recorded as Loss of Pay (LOP), regardless of any later approval.
Clause 2.6: Employees MAY carry forward a maximum of 5 unused annual leave days. Anything above 5 days ARE FORFEITED on 31 December.
Clause 2.7: Carry-forward days MUST be used within January–March, otherwise they are forfeited.
Clause 3.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Each employee is entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per calendar year.
Clause 3.2: Sick leave of 3+ consecutive days REQUIRES a medical certificate submitted within 48 hours of return.
Clause 3.3: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Sick leave cannot be carried forward to the following year.
Clause 3.4: Sick leave adjacent to a public holiday or annual leave REQUIRES a medical certificate unconditionally regardless of duration.
Clause 4.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two live births.
Clause 4.2: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] For a third or subsequent child, maternity leave is 12 weeks paid.
Clause 4.3: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paid paternity leave, to be taken within 30 days of the child's birth.
Clause 4.4: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Paternity leave cannot be split across multiple periods.
Clause 5.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] An employee may apply for Leave Without Pay only after exhausting all applicable paid leave entitlements.
Clause 5.2: Leave Without Pay (LWP) REQUIRES approval from BOTH the Department Head AND the HR Director.
Clause 5.3: LWP exceeding 30 continuous days REQUIRES approval from the Municipal Commissioner.
Clause 5.4: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Periods of LWP do not count toward service for the purposes of seniority, increments, or retirement benefits.
Clause 6.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Employees are entitled to all gazetted public holidays as declared by the State Government each year.
Clause 6.2: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] If an employee is required to work on a public holiday, they are entitled to one compensatory off day, to be taken within 60 days of the holiday worked.
Clause 6.3: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Compensatory off cannot be encashed.
Clause 7.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Annual leave may be encashed only at the time of retirement or resignation, subject to a maximum of 60 days.
Clause 7.2: Leave encashment during active service is NOT PERMITTED under any circumstances.
Clause 7.3: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Sick leave and LWP cannot be encashed under any circumstances.
Clause 8.1: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Leave-related grievances must be raised with the HR Department within 10 working days of the disputed decision.
Clause 8.2: [NEEDS_REVIEW: VERBATIM] Grievances raised after 10 working days will not be considered unless exceptional circumstances are demonstrated in writing.
