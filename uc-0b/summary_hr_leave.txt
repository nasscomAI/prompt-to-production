POLICY SUMMARY — HR LEAVE POLICY (HR-POL-001 v2.3)
Effective: 1 April 2024
=================================================================

--- 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE ---

  Per clause 1.1: This policy governs all leave entitlements for permanent and contractual employees of the City Municipal Corporation (CMC).
  Per clause 1.2: This policy does not apply to daily wage workers or consultants. Those categories are governed by their respective contracts.

--- 2. ANNUAL LEAVE ---

  Per clause 2.1: Each permanent employee is entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
  Per clause 2.2: Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month from the date of joining.
  Per clause 2.3: must submit leave application at least 14 calendar days in advance using Form HR-L1.
  Per clause 2.4: must receive written approval from the employee's direct manager before leave commences. Verbal approval is not valid.
  Per clause 2.5: Unapproved absence will be recorded as Loss of Pay (LOP) regardless of subsequent approval.
  Per clause 2.6: Employees may carry forward a maximum of 5 unused annual leave days to the following calendar year. Any days above 5 are forfeited on 31 December.
  Per clause 2.7: Carry-forward days must be used within the first quarter (January–March) of the following year or they are forfeited.

--- 3. SICK LEAVE ---

  Per clause 3.1: Each employee is entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per calendar year.
  Per clause 3.2: Sick leave of 3 or more consecutive days requires a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner, submitted within 48 hours of returning to work.
  Per clause 3.3: Sick leave cannot be carried forward to the following year.
  Per clause 3.4: Sick leave taken immediately before or after a public holiday or annual leave period requires a medical certificate regardless of duration.

--- 4. MATERNITY AND PATERNITY LEAVE ---

  Per clause 4.1: Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two live births.
  Per clause 4.2: For a third or subsequent child, maternity leave is 12 weeks paid.
  Per clause 4.3: Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paid paternity leave, to be taken within 30 days of the child's birth.
  Per clause 4.4: Paternity leave cannot be split across multiple periods.

--- 5. LEAVE WITHOUT PAY (LWP) ---

  Per clause 5.1: An employee may apply for Leave Without Pay only after exhausting all applicable paid leave entitlements.
  Per clause 5.2: LWP requires approval from BOTH the Department Head AND the HR Director. Manager approval alone is not sufficient.
  Per clause 5.3: LWP exceeding 30 continuous days requires approval from the Municipal Commissioner.
  Per clause 5.4: Periods of LWP do not count toward service for the purposes of seniority, increments, or retirement benefits.

--- 6. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS ---

  Per clause 6.1: Employees are entitled to all gazetted public holidays as declared by the State Government each year.
  Per clause 6.2: If an employee is required to work on a public holiday, they are entitled to one compensatory off day, to be taken within 60 days of the holiday worked.
  Per clause 6.3: Compensatory off cannot be encashed.

--- 7. LEAVE ENCASHMENT ---

  Per clause 7.1: Annual leave may be encashed only at the time of retirement or resignation, subject to a maximum of 60 days.
  Per clause 7.2: Leave encashment during service is not permitted under any circumstances.
  Per clause 7.3: Sick leave and LWP cannot be encashed under any circumstances.

--- 8. GRIEVANCES ---

  Per clause 8.1: Leave-related grievances must be raised with the HR Department within 10 working days of the disputed decision.
  Per clause 8.2: Grievances raised after 10 working days will not be considered unless exceptional circumstances are demonstrated in writing.
