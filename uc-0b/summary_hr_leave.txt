HR LEAVE POLICY — SUMMARY
==================================================

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
----------------------------------------
  [1.1] This policy governs all leave entitlements for permanent and contractual employees.
  [1.2] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] This policy does not apply to daily wage workers or consultants. Those categories are governed by their respective contracts.

2. ANNUAL LEAVE
----------------------------------------
  [2.1] permanent employee receives 18 days of paid annual leave per year.
  [2.2] Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month from joining.
  [2.3] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Employees must submit a leave application at least 14 calendar days in advance using Form HR-L1.
  [2.4] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Leave applications must receive written approval from the employee's direct manager before the leave commences. Verbal approval is not valid.
  [2.5] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Unapproved absence will be recorded as Loss of Pay (LOP) regardless of subsequent approval.
  [2.6] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Employees may carry forward a maximum of 5 unused annual leave days to the following calendar year. Any days above 5 are forfeited on 31 December.
  [2.7] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Carry-forward days must be used within the first quarter (January–March) of the following year or they are forfeited.

3. SICK LEAVE
----------------------------------------
  [3.1] employee receives 12 days of paid sick leave per year.
  [3.2] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Sick leave of 3 or more consecutive days requires a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner, submitted within 48 hours of returning to work.
  [3.3] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Sick leave cannot be carried forward to the next year.
  [3.4] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Sick leave taken immediately before or after a public holiday or annual leave period requires a medical certificate regardless of duration.

4. MATERNITY AND PATERNITY LEAVE
----------------------------------------
  [4.1] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Female employees receive 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two live births.
  [4.2] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] For a third or subsequent child, maternity leave is 12 weeks paid.
  [4.3] Male employees receive 5 days of paid paternity leave, usable within 30 days of birth.
  [4.4] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Paternity leave cannot be split across multiple periods.

5. LEAVE WITHOUT PAY (LWP)
----------------------------------------
  [5.1] An employee may apply for Leave Without Pay only after exhausting all paid leave.
  [5.2] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] LWP requires approval from the Department Head and the HR Director. Manager approval alone is not sufficient.
  [5.3] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] LWP exceeding 30 continuous days requires approval from the Municipal Commissioner.
  [5.4] Periods of LWP do not count toward service toward seniority/increments/retirement.

6. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
----------------------------------------
  [6.1] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Employees receive all gazetted public holidays as declared by the State Government each year.
  [6.2] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] If an employee is required to work on a public holiday, they receive one compensatory off day, usable within 60 days of the holiday worked.
  [6.3] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Compensatory off cannot be encashed.

7. LEAVE ENCASHMENT
----------------------------------------
  [7.1] Annual leave may be encashed only at retirement/resignation, up to 60 days.
  [7.2] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Leave encashment during service is not permitted under any circumstances.
  [7.3] Sick leave and LWP cannot be encashed (no exceptions).

8. GRIEVANCES
----------------------------------------
  [8.1] [VERBATIM — summarisation would alter meaning] Leave-related grievances must be raised with the HR Department within 10 days of the disputed decision.
  [8.2] Grievances raised after 10 days will not be considered unless exceptional circumstances shown in writing.
