HR LEAVE POLICY SUMMARY
=======================
To ensure zero meaning loss, all clauses are extracted verbatim per Rule 4.

[VERBATIM] 1.1 This policy governs all leave entitlements for permanent and contractual employees of the City Municipal Corporation (CMC).
[VERBATIM] 1.2 This policy does not apply to daily wage workers or consultants. Those categories are governed by their respective contracts.
[VERBATIM] 2.1 Each permanent employee is entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
[VERBATIM] 2.2 Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month from the date of joining.
[VERBATIM] 2.3 Employees must submit a leave application at least 14 calendar days in advance using Form HR-L1.
[VERBATIM] 2.4 Leave applications must receive written approval from the employee's direct manager before the leave commences. Verbal approval is not valid.
[VERBATIM] 2.5 Unapproved absence will be recorded as Loss of Pay (LOP) regardless of subsequent approval.
[VERBATIM] 2.6 Employees may carry forward a maximum of 5 unused annual leave days to the following calendar year. Any days above 5 are forfeited on 31 December.
[VERBATIM] 2.7 Carry-forward days must be used within the first quarter (January–March) of the following year or they are forfeited.
[VERBATIM] 3.1 Each employee is entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per calendar year.
[VERBATIM] 3.2 Sick leave of 3 or more consecutive days requires a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner, submitted within 48 hours of returning to work.
[VERBATIM] 3.3 Sick leave cannot be carried forward to the following year.
[VERBATIM] 3.4 Sick leave taken immediately before or after a public holiday or annual leave period requires a medical certificate regardless of duration.
[VERBATIM] 4.1 Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two live births.
[VERBATIM] 4.2 For a third or subsequent child, maternity leave is 12 weeks paid.
[VERBATIM] 4.3 Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paid paternity leave, to be taken within 30 days of the child's birth.
[VERBATIM] 4.4 Paternity leave cannot be split across multiple periods.
[VERBATIM] 5.1 An employee may apply for Leave Without Pay only after exhausting all applicable paid leave entitlements.
[VERBATIM] 5.2 LWP requires approval from the Department Head and the HR Director. Manager approval alone is not sufficient.
[VERBATIM] 5.3 LWP exceeding 30 continuous days requires approval from the Municipal Commissioner.
[VERBATIM] 5.4 Periods of LWP do not count toward service for the purposes of seniority, increments, or retirement benefits.
[VERBATIM] 6.1 Employees are entitled to all gazetted public holidays as declared by the State Government each year.
[VERBATIM] 6.2 If an employee is required to work on a public holiday, they are entitled to one compensatory off day, to be taken within 60 days of the holiday worked.
[VERBATIM] 6.3 Compensatory off cannot be encashed.
[VERBATIM] 7.1 Annual leave may be encashed only at the time of retirement or resignation, subject to a maximum of 60 days.
[VERBATIM] 7.2 Leave encashment during service is not permitted under any circumstances.
[VERBATIM] 7.3 Sick leave and LWP cannot be encashed under any circumstances.
[VERBATIM] 8.1 Leave-related grievances must be raised with the HR Department within 10 working days of the disputed decision.
[VERBATIM] 8.2 Grievances raised after 10 working days will not be considered unless exceptional circumstances are demonstrated in writing.
