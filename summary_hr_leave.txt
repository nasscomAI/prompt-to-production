SECTION — HR LEAVE POLICY
• Version 3.2 | Effective Date: 01 January 2024
• Issued by: Human Resources Department

SECTION — 1. SCOPE
• 1.1 This policy applies to all permanent employees of the organisation.
• 1.2 Contract employees and interns are governed by separate agreements.
• 1.3 This policy supersedes all previous leave policy versions.

SECTION — 2. ANNUAL LEAVE
• [NUMERIC] 2.1 Permanent employees are entitled to 21 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
• 2.2 Leave must be applied for at least 5 business days in advance using the HR portal.
• [NUMERIC] 2.3 Unused annual leave may be carried forward by a maximum of 5 days per year.
• [NUMERIC] 2.4 Any unused leave beyond 5 days will lapse at the end of the calendar year and will not be encashed.
• [PENALTY] 2.5 Employees who resign must take or forfeit their remaining annual leave; no encashment will be permitted upon resignation.

SECTION — 3. SICK LEAVE
• [NUMERIC] 3.1 Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per calendar year.
• 3.2 Sick leave does not carry forward to the following year.
• 3.3 A medical certificate from a registered practitioner is required for any sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days.
• 3.4 Sick leave taken without a medical certificate beyond the 12-day entitlement will be treated as unpaid leave.
• [PENALTY] 3.5 Falsification of medical certificates will result in immediate termination of employment.

SECTION — 4. MATERNITY AND PATERNITY LEAVE
• [NUMERIC] 4.1 Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two children.
• [NUMERIC] 4.2 For a third or subsequent child, maternity leave is reduced to 12 weeks paid.
• [NUMERIC] 4.3 Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paid paternity leave, to be taken within 6 weeks of the child's birth.
• [NUMERIC] 4.4 Paternity leave not taken within 6 weeks will lapse and cannot be claimed thereafter.

SECTION — 5. EMERGENCY LEAVE
• [NUMERIC] 5.1 Up to 3 days of emergency leave may be granted per year for unforeseen personal crises.
• 5.2 Emergency leave must be approved retrospectively by the employee's direct manager within 24 hours of return.
• [PENALTY] 5.3 Emergency leave is paid and will not be deducted from annual or sick leave entitlement.
• [PENALTY] [EXCEPTION] 5.4 Repeated misuse of emergency leave provisions will be subject to disciplinary action.

SECTION — 6. LEAVE WITHOUT PAY
• 6.1 Employees may apply for unpaid leave for personal reasons beyond their paid entitlements.
• 6.2 Unpaid leave must be approved by both the direct manager and the HR department.
• 6.3 Unpaid leave in excess of 30 calendar days will result in a proportional reduction of annual leave accrual for that year.

SECTION — 7. ABANDONMENT OF EMPLOYMENT
• 7.1 An employee who is absent for 5 or more consecutive business days without approval or communication will be deemed to have abandoned their employment.
• [PENALTY] 7.2 Abandonment of employment will result in summary dismissal without notice pay.
• [PENALTY] 7.3 The organisation will attempt to contact the employee via registered post before issuing a dismissal notice.
