SUMMARY — HR LEAVE POLICY  (NasscomAI Civic Tech, v2.1)
Effective: 01 January 2025
================================================================

PURPOSE & SCOPE
───────────────
This policy governs leave entitlements, eligibility, approval, and
penalties for all full-time and part-time employees. Permanent,
contractual, and probationary staff are covered. Consultants and
interns are NOT eligible for paid leave.

LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS AT A GLANCE
───────────────────────────────
  Type                       Entitlement      Carry Fwd        Encashment
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Earned Leave (EL)          18 days/year     30 days          10 days/year (not during notice period)
  Sick Leave (SL)            12 days/year     Nil — lapses     Not permitted
  Casual Leave (CL)          6 days/year      Nil — lapses     Not permitted
  Maternity Leave (ML)       26 weeks paid    N/A              N/A
  Paternity Leave (PL)       5 days paid      N/A              N/A
  Bereavement Leave (BL)     3 days (imm.)    N/A              N/A
  Comp-Off                   As earned        60 days          Not permitted

CLAUSE-BY-CLAUSE DETAIL
────────────────────────

3.1 EARNED LEAVE
  • 18 days per calendar year for confirmed employees only.
  • Accrual: 1.5 days/month from date of confirmation.
  • Probationary employees are NOT eligible.
  • Maximum carry forward: 30 days. Balance above 30 days LAPSES at year-end.
  • Encashment: maximum 10 days per year during appraisal cycle.
    ⚠ Encashment is NOT permitted during notice period.
  • More than 5 consecutive EL days requires minimum 12 months of service.

3.2 SICK LEAVE
  • 12 days per calendar year.
  • Unused SL LAPSES — no carry forward permitted.
  • Medical certificate from a registered practitioner is MANDATORY
    for sick leave exceeding 3 consecutive days.
  • Combining SL with EL requires prior WRITTEN approval from HR Head.

3.3 CASUAL LEAVE
  • 6 days per calendar year.
  • Cannot be carried forward or encashed under ANY circumstances.
  • Maximum 2 consecutive CL days at one time.
  • Cannot be prefixed/suffixed to a public holiday without manager approval.

3.4 MATERNITY LEAVE
  • 26 weeks (182 days) fully paid — as per Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (amended 2017).
  • Requires minimum 80 days of work in the 12 months before expected delivery.
  • Additional 12 weeks of UNPAID leave may be requested with written approval
    from Department Head AND HR Director.
  • Adoptive mothers: 12 weeks paid leave for children below 3 months of age.

3.5 PATERNITY LEAVE
  • 5 days paid leave within 6 months of childbirth or adoption.
  • Cannot be split into more than 2 spells.

3.6 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
  • Immediate family (spouse, children, parents, siblings): 3 days.
  • Extended family (grandparents, in-laws): 1 day.
  • Must be applied within 7 days of the event.
  • Supporting documentation (death certificate or equivalent) is MANDATORY.

3.7 COMPENSATORY OFF
  • Awarded for working on declared public holidays or weekends (manager instruction).
  • Must be availed within 60 days. Unused comp-off LAPSES — NOT encashable.
  • Maximum 10 comp-off days accumulated at any time.

LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS
──────────────────────────
  • Earned Leave   → minimum 5 working days advance notice.
  • Casual Leave   → minimum 1 working day advance notice.
  • Emergency leave → notify manager within 2 hours of shift start.

  Approval hierarchy (MANDATORY):
    Up to 3 days      → Direct Manager
    4–7 days          → Direct Manager + HR Business Partner
    More than 7 days  → Direct Manager + HR Business Partner + Department Head
  ⚠ Leave taken without approval = Loss of Pay (LOP).

  All applications MUST be submitted via the HRMS portal.
  Email or verbal requests are NOT valid.

LEAVE WITHOUT PAY (LWP)
────────────────────────
  • Requires same approval hierarchy as above.
  • More than 10 LWP days → proportional deduction in annual increment and bonus.
  • More than 30 LWP days → treated as MISCONDUCT; may lead to termination.

PENALTIES & DISCIPLINARY ACTION
─────────────────────────────────
  • 1–2 days unauthorised absence       → Written warning.
  • 3–5 days unauthorised absence       → Salary deduction + formal warning.
  • More than 5 days unauthorised absence → Treated as VOLUNTARY ABANDONMENT
                                            and may result in TERMINATION without notice.
  • False medical certificates / fraudulent applications → Immediate disciplinary
    action, may lead to TERMINATION.

PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
────────────────
  • 12 public holidays per year (declared annually by HR in January).
  • Working on a mandatory holiday requires Department Head approval
    and qualifies for Comp-Off (Section 3.7).

POLICY AMENDMENTS
──────────────────
  • Reviewed annually by HR Director.
  • Changes communicated with 30 days' notice.
  • Exceptions MUST be approved in writing by HR Director.
    Verbal exceptions are NOT valid.
