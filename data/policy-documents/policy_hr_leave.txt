CMC Employee Leave Policy Summary (Effective 1 April 2024)

This policy applies to permanent and contractual employees of the City Municipal Corporation. It does not apply to daily wage workers or consultants.

Annual Leave:
- Permanent employees receive 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year.
- Annual leave accrues at 1.5 days per month from the date of joining.
- Employees must apply at least 14 calendar days in advance using Form HR-L1.
- Leave must be approved in writing by the direct manager before the leave starts. Verbal approval is not valid.
- Unapproved absence is treated as Loss of Pay (LOP), even if approval is obtained later.
- A maximum of 5 unused annual leave days can be carried forward.
- Carried-forward leave must be used between January and March of the next year or it is forfeited.

Sick Leave:
- Employees receive 12 days of paid sick leave per calendar year.
- Sick leave for 3 or more consecutive days requires a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner.
- The certificate must be submitted within 48 hours of returning to work.
- Sick leave cannot be carried forward.
- Sick leave taken immediately before or after a public holiday or annual leave also requires a medical certificate, regardless of duration.

Maternity and Paternity Leave:
- Female employees are entitled to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two live births.
- For a third or later child, maternity leave is 12 weeks paid.
- Male employees are entitled to 5 days of paid paternity leave.
- Paternity leave must be taken within 30 days of the child’s birth and cannot be split across multiple periods.

Leave Without Pay (LWP):
- Employees may apply for LWP only after exhausting all applicable paid leave.
- LWP requires approval from both the Department Head and the HR Director.
- Manager approval alone is not sufficient.
- LWP of more than 30 continuous days also requires approval from the Municipal Commissioner.
- LWP does not count toward seniority, increments, or retirement benefits.

Public Holidays:
- Employees are entitled to gazetted public holidays declared by the State Government.
- If required to work on a public holiday, the employee gets one compensatory off day.
- The compensatory off must be taken within 60 days.
- Compensatory off cannot be encashed.

Leave Encashment:
- Annual leave can be encashed only at retirement or resignation, up to 60 days.
- Leave encashment during service is not allowed.
- Sick leave and LWP cannot be encashed.

Grievances:
- Leave-related grievances must be raised with HR within 10 working days of the disputed decision.
- Complaints raised after 10 working days will not be considered unless exceptional circumstances are provided in writing.
