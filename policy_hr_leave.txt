HR Leave Policy — Municipal Corporation of Hyderabad
Effective Date: 1 April 2024
Version: 2.1

Section 1: Scope
1.1 This policy applies to all permanent employees of the Municipal Corporation of Hyderabad (MCH), including full-time staff, contract employees on terms of 12 months or more, and deputed officers.
1.2 Temporary staff engaged for less than 90 days are excluded from this policy.

Section 2: Types of Leave
2.1 Earned Leave (EL): Each permanent employee shall be entitled to 30 days of earned leave per calendar year, accrued at a rate of 2.5 days per month.
2.2 Sick Leave (SL): Each employee shall be entitled to 12 days of sick leave per calendar year. Sick leave beyond 3 consecutive days must be supported by a registered medical practitioner's certificate.
2.3 Casual Leave (CL): Each employee shall be entitled to 12 days of casual leave per calendar year. Casual leave must not be taken for more than 3 consecutive days at a time.
2.4 Maternity Leave: Female employees who have completed 12 months of continuous service shall be entitled to 180 days of paid maternity leave. This entitlement applies for a maximum of 2 pregnancies.
2.5 Paternity Leave: Male employees shall be entitled to 15 days of paid paternity leave within 6 months of childbirth. Paternity leave not availed within 6 months of childbirth shall lapse and cannot be carried forward.

Section 3: Leave Accrual and Carry-Forward
3.1 Earned leave shall be credited to the employee's leave account on the first working day of each calendar year.
3.2 A maximum of 60 days of earned leave may be carried forward to the next calendar year. Leave in excess of 60 days shall lapse and will not be encashed.
3.3 Sick leave and casual leave shall not be carried forward. Any unused balance at the end of the calendar year shall lapse without encashment.

Section 4: Leave Application Process
4.1 Employees must apply for leave using the approved HR portal at least 5 working days in advance for earned leave. Applications submitted with less than 5 working days' notice may be rejected at the discretion of the reporting officer.
4.2 Sick leave must be applied for within 24 hours of the employee's absence. Failure to apply within 24 hours will result in the leave being marked as Leave Without Pay (LWP).
4.3 All leave applications must be approved by the employee's direct reporting officer before the leave commences. Leave taken without prior approval shall be treated as unauthorised absence.

Section 5: Leave Without Pay (LWP)
5.1 Leave Without Pay shall be granted only in exceptional circumstances and must be approved by the Head of Department.
5.2 LWP shall not be granted for more than 30 consecutive days in a calendar year without approval from the Commissioner.
5.3 Periods of LWP will not count toward earned leave accrual. An employee on LWP for more than 30 days in a year shall have their annual EL entitlement reduced proportionately.

Section 6: Penalties and Disciplinary Provisions
6.1 Employees who are absent for more than 10 consecutive working days without approved leave or communication shall be issued a show-cause notice.
6.2 Employees who fail to respond to a show-cause notice within 7 days of issuance shall be liable to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of service.
6.3 Employees who misrepresent medical conditions to obtain sick leave shall be subject to immediate suspension and subsequent disciplinary proceedings, which may result in termination.
6.4 Encashment of leave based on false records shall be treated as financial misconduct and referred to the Vigilance Department.

Section 7: Public Holidays
7.1 All gazetted public holidays declared by the Government of Telangana shall be observed by MCH employees.
7.2 Employees required to work on a public holiday shall be entitled to a compensatory off day to be availed within 30 days of the public holiday worked. Compensatory off not availed within 30 days shall lapse.

Section 8: Amendment and Review
8.1 This policy shall be reviewed annually by the HR Department.
8.2 Amendments to this policy shall be approved by the Commissioner and communicated to all employees at least 30 days before the effective date of the amendment.
